From MUSIC III to Csound 6.12

57 years of Computer Music Languages

Victor Lazzarini

Introduction

In November 2018, Csound version 6.12 was released. This marks the final stage of the 6-series of the software. As we move towards version 7, we can trace back the 57 years of development and the body of knowledge that precedes it.

Max Mathews

Programming music and audio in digital computers started with Max Mathews and his group at Bell Labs in 1957.

Two developments were key:

  • direct digital synthesis by software with MUSIC I and MUSIC II;
  • a practical DAC to convert digital audio from computer tapes.

Roots: MUSIC III (1961)

MUSIC III introduced the idea of an 'acoustic compiler'.

It also introduced the concept of the unit generator, a central component of music programming languages.


Trunk: MUSIC IV (1964)

MUSIC IV introduced a domain-specific language for music and a number of other unit generators.

A more complex system, allowed the use of Fortran-coded routines for event manipulation.

Branches

MUSIC IV was the model for a great number of systems that were developed in the following decades.

MUSIC IVB, MUSIC IVBF, MUSIC 4C, MUSIC 10 MUSIC V, CMUSIC, CMIX, CLM
